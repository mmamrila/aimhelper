üöÄ Getting Started

How do I start using AimHelper Pro?

Simply visit our platform, set up your gaming profile with your current DPI and sensitivity, then complete our 4 performance tests. No account required to get started!

Do I need to create an account?

No! You can use AimHelper Pro completely anonymously. However, creating an account allows you to save your results, track progress over time, and access advanced analytics.

How long does the analysis take?

The complete analysis takes about 5-10 minutes. Each test runs for 30-60 seconds, and you'll get instant results as soon as you complete them.

üéØ Testing Process

What are the 4 tests and what do they measure?

Circle Tracking: Measures smooth tracking and micro-adjustments

Grid Shot: Tests precision clicking and target acquisition

Flick Test: Evaluates quick target-to-target movements

Consistency Test: Analyzes muscle memory and repeatability

Do I need to complete all tests?

While you can get basic recommendations with 2+ tests, completing all 4 tests provides the most accurate analysis. Each test evaluates different aspects of your aiming ability.

Can I retake tests to improve my results?

Yes! You can retake any test as many times as you want. Our algorithm uses your best performances to calculate optimal settings, so more attempts can lead to better recommendations.

What if my mouse or setup changes during testing?

For best results, maintain consistent setup throughout testing. If you change DPI or sensitivity mid-session, update your profile settings before continuing with additional tests.

üìä Results & Recommendations

How accurate are the sensitivity recommendations?

Our recommendations are based on your actual performance data and game-specific optimization algorithms. Confidence scores indicate reliability - higher scores (85%+) suggest very accurate recommendations.

Why might my recommended sensitivity be different from what I expect?

Our analysis is based on your actual performance, not preferences or popular settings. The recommendation might differ because it's optimized for your natural aiming patterns and the specific game you selected.

What does the confidence score mean?

85-100%: High confidence - strong sensitivity preference identified

65-84%: Good confidence - clear performance patterns detected

Below 65%: Lower confidence - consider additional testing

Should I change my sensitivity immediately?

Large sensitivity changes should be made gradually. If the recommendation differs significantly from your current setting, adjust slowly over several gaming sessions to avoid disrupting muscle memory.

What's the difference between DPI and sensitivity?

DPI: Mouse hardware setting (dots per inch)

Sensitivity: In-game multiplier setting

in/360¬∞: Physical distance needed for a full turn (combines both DPI and sensitivity)

Can I use this on different monitors or resolutions?

Yes! Our tests work across different screen sizes and resolutions. The recommendations are based on mouse movement distance (in/360¬∞), which remains consistent regardless of display.

üîß Troubleshooting

Tests feel laggy or unresponsive

Ensure you're using a modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari), close other demanding applications, and make sure your mouse drivers are up to date. Wired mice typically provide better performance than wireless.

My results seem inconsistent

Consistency improves with practice. Ensure you're warmed up, maintain the same sitting position and grip, and avoid testing when tired. Taking multiple test sessions can help identify your true optimal range.

Page won't load or tests won't start

Try refreshing the page, clearing your browser cache, or disabling browser extensions. Make sure JavaScript is enabled. If issues persist, try a different browser.
