Pixel Skipping

When sensitivity is too low, your crosshair "jumps" over pixels instead of smoothly transitioning, affecting precision.

Prevention: Maintain reasonable DPI (400+ recommended)

üîÑ Finding Your Baseline

  1. Start with 30 cm/360¬∞ as a baseline
  2. Test for 1-2 weeks consistently
  3. Adjust in small increments (2-5 cm/360¬∞)
  4. Focus on comfort and consistency

üß† Muscle Memory Development

  1. Stick to one sensitivity for at least 2 weeks
  2. Practice aim training daily (15-30 minutes)
  3. Focus on smooth, controlled movements
  4. Track your progress with consistent metrics

‚öñÔ∏è DPI vs In-Game Balance

  1. Use your mouse's native DPI when possible
  2. Avoid extremely low in-game sensitivity (<0.1)
  3. Higher DPI reduces input lag slightly
  4. Most pros use 400-1600 DPI range

üñ±Ô∏è Hardware Considerations

  1. Ensure adequate mousepad space
  2. Use a consistent mouse surface
  3. Keep your mouse sensor clean
  4. Consider mouse weight for comfort

‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

Constantly Changing Sensitivity

Switching sensitivity frequently prevents muscle memory development. Commit to a setting for at least 2-3 weeks.

Copying Pro Settings Exactly

Professional players' settings are optimized for their playstyle, hardware, and physical attributes. Use them as a starting point, not a rule.

Ignoring Hardware Limitations

Your mousepad size, desk setup, and physical comfort should influence your sensitivity choice more than arbitrary numbers.

Focusing Only on Flick Shots

Tracking and micro-adjustments are equally important. A balanced sensitivity helps with all aim scenarios.
